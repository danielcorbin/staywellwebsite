Peptides We Work With

Here are examples of the types of peptides used across our programs. Which are appropriate for you depends on your individual goals, health history, and lab findings.

GH Secretagogue

Sermorelin / Ipamorelin / CJC-1295

These peptides stimulate your pituitary gland to produce and release your own growth hormone, rather than replacing it directly. The result is a more physiologic increase in GH and IGF-1 with a favorable safety profile.

  • Improved sleep quality and recovery
  • Lean muscle preservation and growth
  • Reduction in visceral fat
  • Enhanced energy and cognitive clarity
Repair & Recovery

BPC-157

Body Protective Compound 157 is a synthetic peptide derived from a protein found in gastric juice. It has shown remarkable tissue-healing and anti-inflammatory properties across multiple systems in the body.

  • Accelerated tendon and ligament repair
  • Gut lining support and GI healing
  • Reduced systemic inflammation
  • Neuroprotective and joint-supportive effects
Cellular Energy

NAD+ / NMN

NAD+ is a coenzyme essential to cellular energy production and DNA repair. Levels decline significantly with age. Restoring NAD+ levels is one of the most well-researched strategies for supporting cellular longevity.

  • Mitochondrial function and energy production
  • DNA repair and cellular resilience
  • Cognitive function and neuroprotection
  • Metabolic regulation and inflammation reduction
Tissue Healing

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)

TB-500 promotes the regulation of actin, a protein critical to cell structure and movement. It plays a key role in wound healing, muscle repair, and reducing inflammation throughout the body.

  • Accelerated healing of muscle, tendon, and connective tissue
  • Reduced inflammation at injury sites
  • Improved flexibility and range of motion
  • Often combined with BPC-157 for synergistic repair effects
A note on peptide availability: The regulatory landscape for peptide therapy continues to evolve. Not all peptides are currently available through compounding pharmacies, and availability may change over time. Your provider will discuss which options are currently available and appropriate for you at your consultation.
