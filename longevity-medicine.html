Anti-Aging & Longevity Medicine

Not Just Longer

Anti-aging and longevity medicine at Staywell goes beyond managing disease. We use evidence-informed therapies, including peptide therapy, hormonal optimization, and metabolic health strategies, to slow cellular aging, restore function, and help you thrive well into late life.

Medicine Has Been Focused on the Wrong Question

For decades, conventional medicine has asked: how do we treat disease after it appears? Longevity medicine asks a different question: how do we preserve function, slow biological aging, and extend the years you actually feel well? The research is now clear that aging itself is a modifiable process - and that interventions started in your 30s, 40s, and 50s can meaningfully change your trajectory in your 60s, 70s, and beyond.

A Different Kind of Care

Longevity medicine doesn't replace your primary care - it expands what's possible within it.

⚠ Conventional Model

  • Wait for symptoms before acting
  • Treat disease after it has developed
  • Focus on managing chronic conditions
  • Reference ranges designed around average, not optimal
  • Aging treated as inevitable decline
  • Fragmented care across specialists

✓ The Longevity Approach

  • Identify and address risk before symptoms appear
  • Support the body's own regenerative capacity
  • Optimize function at every stage of life
  • Interpret biomarkers in context of what is optimal for you
  • Treat aging as a process that can be slowed
  • Integrated care with your full health picture in mind

Three Pillars of Longevity Medicine

Our longevity programs are built around three interconnected areas that collectively drive how well - and how long - you feel like yourself.

Cellular Regeneration

Peptide therapies & repair protocols

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules, directing your body to repair tissue, reduce inflammation, stimulate growth hormone release, and support cellular health. Unlike synthetic drugs, many peptides work by amplifying processes your body already knows how to do.

  • Growth hormone secretagogues to support lean mass and recovery
  • Repair peptides targeting inflammation and tissue healing
  • Protocols tailored to your specific goals and biology
  • Ongoing monitoring to assess response and adjust

Hormonal Optimization

Bioidentical HRT & micronutrient support

Hormones decline with age - often silently, and often years before symptoms become obvious. Restoring and maintaining optimal hormonal balance is one of the most impactful interventions available for energy, body composition, brain function, and long-term vitality.

  • Comprehensive hormone and micronutrient lab review
  • Bioidentical testosterone, estrogen, progesterone protocols
  • Thyroid and adrenal optimization
  • Micronutrient repletion as a foundation for hormone function

Metabolic Health

Body composition, glucose & inflammation

Metabolic dysfunction - insulin resistance, chronic low-grade inflammation, visceral fat accumulation - is the common thread running through most age-related disease. Addressing it early and aggressively is central to a longevity strategy that actually works.

  • Advanced metabolic and inflammatory marker assessment
  • Medical weight loss and body composition optimization
  • Nutrition and movement guidance grounded in metabolic science
  • Targeted interventions for insulin resistance and glucose regulation

Peptide Therapy as Part of Your Protocol

Peptide therapy is one of the tools we draw on within a longevity protocol, alongside hormonal and metabolic strategies. Which peptides make sense for you depends on your goals, health history, and lab findings.

Who Longevity Medicine Is For

You do not have to be sick to benefit from longevity medicine. Most of our longevity patients are functioning well - but want to stay that way for as long as possible.

  • Adults in their 30s–60s interested in proactive health optimization
  • People experiencing unexplained fatigue, cognitive changes, or body composition shifts
  • Athletes or active adults seeking enhanced recovery and performance
  • Those with a family history of metabolic, cardiovascular, or neurodegenerative disease
  • Anyone who has been told their labs are "normal" but does not feel normal
  • Patients looking for a provider who takes a long-term, integrated view of their health
