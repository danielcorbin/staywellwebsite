Your Labs Came Back "Normal." But You Don't Feel Normal.

Standard reference ranges are built around population averages, not optimal function. Testosterone in the low-normal range gets dismissed, even when it's robbing you of drive, strength, and sleep. A TSH of 3.8 is technically within range, but for many men it represents sluggish thyroid function that affects energy and weight too. At Staywell, we read labs the way they were meant to be read, in the context of your symptoms, your history, and what optimal actually looks like for you.

Recognizing the Signs

Low testosterone doesn't always announce itself clearly. It often shows up as a slow drift away from who you used to be, until one day you realize something is off.

Common Symptoms of Low Testosterone

  • Low energy and motivation, even with adequate sleep
  • Declining strength or difficulty building muscle
  • Reduced libido or erectile dysfunction
  • Brain fog and poor concentration
  • Increased body fat, especially visceral
  • Mood changes: irritability, low drive, flat affect
  • Poor sleep quality or insomnia
  • Hair loss or skin changes
  • Andropause, a gradual decline in testosterone with age

If several of these resonate with you, your hormones may be worth a closer look, even if prior bloodwork was called "normal."

The Staywell Approach to Men's Hormone Care

We don't hand you a standard protocol and send you on your way. Hormone optimization at Staywell is a supervised, evolving process built around your individual response.

Expert Lab Interpretation

We review a comprehensive hormone and micronutrient panel and interpret results against optimal ranges, not just population averages. Your numbers are read in context of your symptoms and goals.

Ongoing Monitoring

Regular follow-up labs and provider check-ins ensure your protocol is working and allows for precise adjustments as your body responds. This is a process, not a one-time prescription.

Whole-Person Context

Sleep, stress, nutrition, and lifestyle all influence hormone function. Your protocol is designed with all of these in mind, not just the numbers on your lab report.

What to Expect

Hormone optimization is a supervised process, not a quick fix. Here is what the journey typically looks like.

1

Intake Consultation

Your provider reviews your full health history, current symptoms, medications, and goals. This conversation shapes the lab panel ordered for you, not a generic checklist, but a targeted picture of your hormonal and metabolic health.

2

Comprehensive Lab Review

We run a thorough hormone panel that may include testosterone, thyroid markers, adrenal function, micronutrients, and metabolic markers. Results are interpreted by your provider in context, not just flagged as in or out of range.

3

Your Individualized Protocol

Based on your labs, symptoms, and goals, your provider builds a personalized protocol. This may include testosterone replacement therapy, micronutrient supplementation, lifestyle adjustments, or a combination. Dosing is precise and compounded specifically for you.

4

Follow-Up and Refinement

Hormone optimization takes time. Follow-up labs and regular check-ins allow your provider to track your response, make adjustments, and ensure you are moving toward feeling genuinely well, not just hitting a number on paper.

5

Long-Term Optimization

Once you reach a stable, optimal baseline, the focus shifts to maintaining it. Your provider continues to monitor labs over time and adjust as your body changes with age, stress, or life circumstances.
